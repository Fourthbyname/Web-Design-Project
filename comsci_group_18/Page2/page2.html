Rock and Roll

About

Rock and Roll (Often shortened to Rock) has, since its mainstream breakthrough in the 1950's, consistently been one of the most popular genres of popular music. A unique fusion of genres such as blues, jazz, and country just to name a few, the rebellious spirit of rock and roll music has captured the minds of generations of people. From the sneering vocals of Mick Jagger, to the crashing drum fills of John Bonham, to the iconic slap of a Flea bass line, to David Gilmour's roaring guitar solos, this is a genre that has truly has something to offer for everyone.


Characteristics

The iconic rock and roll sound is a product of the core characteristics of the music within the genre. The electric guitar, which when combined with high-gain amplifiers and effects such as distortion, is a critical part of the rock and roll sound. Electric guitars would become the defining instrument of rock, often used as both a lead instrument, giving us those roaring guitar solos we associate with many rock hits, or as a rhythmic instrument, providing backing to a singer or a lead guitarist. Vocals are typically harsh, but full of emotion and expression. Many rock classics are defined by the high-pitched wails of singers such as Robert Plant or Axl Rose. Lyrically, rock vocalists often sing about themes associated with rebellion, love and the struggles of the working class population. To tie this all together a strong rhythmic backbone is key. The aforementioned electric guitar is often a part of this, along with bass and drums. Upright bass was used in rock's earliest days to provide a strong low-end. The bass guitar, similar to a traditional stringed bass but much smaller in size and intended to be played horizontally, would soon replace upright bass as the go-to instrument in providing bass. Combined with the electrification seen on the traditional guitar, bass could take advantage of many of the benefits of the electric guitar too. Finally, there's the drums, responsible for providing a strong, driving backbeat.
