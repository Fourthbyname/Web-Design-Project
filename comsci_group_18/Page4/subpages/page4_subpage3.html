Tupac Shakur (2Pac)

Pac’s whole appeal goes way beyond music, he had this raw emotional honesty mixed with poetic social commentary. One track he could make you hype, the next he’d hit you with something reflective or heartbreaking.

The Notorious B.I.G

Biggie had that smooth, effortless flow that just felt untouchable. His storytelling was cinematic, you felt like you were sitting right there in Brooklyn with him.

Jay-Z

Longevity, consistency, and growth. Jay’s career feels like a blueprint for every rapper with big ambitions. He’s evolved from street narratives to reflective, mature bars without losing that edge.

Eminem

Whether you like his style or not, nobody can deny the technical ability. The rhyming, the speed, the emotional range, he pretty much changed the landscape when he arrived.
